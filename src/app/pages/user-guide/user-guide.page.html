Getting Started
Wash Before Use

Wash before first use. We recommend laundering the masks daily after use. The materials we've used can withstand 190F temperatures. DO NOT wash the filter. Always remove the filter before washing.
Wash Your Hands

Always wash your hands with soap and warm water for at least 20-seconds - before AND after you handle the mask.
Insert Filter

The Tour Mask is compatible with standard N95 / PM2.5 filters.Filters are disposable, and should be changed frequently.

1) To Insert the disposable filter, roll the top and bottom edges of the filter media into a cylinder.

2) Carefully begin to insert the mask through the side opening, on the inside of the mask.

3) Continue until the filter is centered between the top nose and bottom chin darts.

4) Check to ensure the filter is correctly positioned in the mask.
Fitting the Mask

Cover Your Nose & Mouth

Place the mask so that it covers your nose and mouth. We recommend that the mesh side faces outwards.

Pinch the Nose Strip

Pinch the shapeable aluminum nose strip to help form a better seal under the eyes and around your nose bridge.

Reversing the Mask

Reversing the mask (so the white side faces outwards) can help with breathability - for extended daily wear. We DO NOT recommend this option for use in public places. Read the FAQs for How To Reverse the Mask.

Removing the Mask

1) Wash your hands - then carefully pull the straps over your head. Use the straps to pull the mask away from your face.

2) Never touch the mask as you risk contaminating yourself - keep your hands off of your face and use common sense.
Removing the Filter

1) Change the filter as frequently as possible, ideally daily. The filter can last for up to 7-days depending on the manufacturer.

2) Always discard the filter in a safe place immediately after removing it from the mask - do not leave the filter lying around.
